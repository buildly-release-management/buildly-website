The Technical Co-Founder Dilemma

Finding a great technical co-founder is one of the biggest pain points for non-technical founders. Here's why:

1. High Demand, Limited Supply

Skilled developers are in high demand and often prefer high-paying jobs at established companies over the risk of an early-stage startup.

2. Evaluating Technical Skills is Hard

If you're not technical yourself, how do you know if a developer is truly qualified? Without the right vetting process, many founders end up partnering with developers who can't deliver.

3. Mismatched Expectations

  • Time commitment: Some developers prefer side projects, while you need someone all-in.
  • Equity vs. salary: Startups often can't afford full salaries, but many technical partners prefer cash over equity.
  • Long-term vision: If your co-founder doesn't share your passion, they might walk away when things get tough.

4. The Cost of a Bad Match

A poor co-founder match can set your startup back by months (or even years)‚Äîburning money, delaying product launches, and damaging investor trust.
